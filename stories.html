Harbor, First Light

The day began before the city woke. I waited as the water took on color—gray to blue, then a thin line of amber across the hulls. The photo is less about boats than the quiet breath between them.

Rain on the Third Floor

I framed the window and waited for the bus to pass. The glass turned the scene into a painting—streaks of color, a pause in the schedule. The moment was borrowed, and that made it honest.

Noon on the Long Road

Harsh light leaves nothing to hide. The lines felt ceremonial— a procession of shadows keeping time across the shoulder.
